Company projects

Feb.2024 - Mar.2024. CAPI stands for Computer Assisted Personal Interviewing. In other words, it's one of the methods of data collection that takes place face-to-face, with the interviewer on one side with the form in his tablet, and the interviewee on the other. Socio is the Louvre's partner for visitor surveys. In concrete terms, the interviewers use our form to conduct interviews. The data team then processes and analyzes the data. The aim of the surveys is to gain a better understanding of visitors, in order to attract more of them. However, it is not possible to conduct interviews without an internet connection. This is a problem, as some places have very poor or no connection at all. This costs us time, interviews, and potentially representativeness. I'm here to solve this problem, the goal is to implement a feature enabling connectless interviews. For this, I use local browser storage to save responses.
And I'm using PWA technology to turn our questionnaire into an application. I code in Javascript, html, and css. After testing a number of approaches, I've created a page that contains the interview table, with the option of taking over an interview, deleting it and sending it to the server. This project allowed me to surpass myself, as I learned to code in Javascript, and to strengthen my ability to search and debug.

Feb.2023-Jun.2023. With my group of 4 people, I thought about a problem based on the data made available to me. This study is based on data from a database provided by the Observational Study in Clinical Epidemiology of 5 US hospitals that applied the RHC method. It concerns 5,735 patients over 18 years of age hospitalized in intensive care between June 1989 and January 1994. RHC, or right heart catheterization, consists in placing a Swan Ganz catheter to resuscitate a patient in serious condition. I wanted to see if this treatment, applied to intensive care patients, was beneficial, if it could save the most people, and more specifically to see if the RHC method was relevant and necessary in certain situations.
The goal was to produce a technical report, a statistical report, a project management report, and a final presentation of the results with slides. The technical report contains the approach I took on R to clean and sort our data. Indeed, it was necessary to understand and assimilate the data. After cleaning, my observations went from 5735 patients to 5508 patients. For example, I removed observations that had outliers on a variable. I decided to delete a variable that had a lot of missing values. I also grouped variables into one for easier manipulation later on, etc.
Then I made graphs and analyses where I added it to the statistical report. And I presented our results on Powerpoint during the oral presentation. Throughout the project, I drew up a project management report, which consisted in planning the project with a Gantt chart, organizing it, and seeing whether the expected time and resources had been respected.
I used R software for data processing and analysis. This project taught me a lot about teamwork, organization and autonomy.

May.2023 - Jun.2023. Within the LAC distribution group (Leclerc, Auchan, Carrefour), our team (of 4 people) aims to structure data by implementing a database for decision-making purposes. Here, we are looking to monitor forecasting in the fruit and vegetable food sector. First, we had to create the conceptual and logical data model.
Then we cleaned and reformatted the data. We created the database using queries on Access. We also identified any anomalies. After calculating productivity and the rate of change, we were able to conclude whether or not to allocate headcount to the various sites.
I was the group leader. Moreover, we produced the best project of the promo. It wasn't always easy and straightforward. After all, we hadn't quite mastered Access at the time. It took a lot of trial and error to get to the end. There were a lot of queries to be made, which can quickly get us lost. We also have other projects at the same time, which doesn't leave us much time for rest and relaxation. But, thinking about the conceptual data model, implementing the database, and finding an answer to the problem based on this data are interesting and rewarding tasks. Because it prepares us for the eventual missions we may encounter in the professional world.
